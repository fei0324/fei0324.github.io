Fangfei Lan

I am a PhD student at the Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute (SCI), University of Utah, working with Dr. Bei Wang on topological data analysis and data visualization. I study topology because I find objects such as the mobius strip and the Klein bottle fascinating, and also because most objects have geometrical properties and I believe that shape matters. My research goal is to use the theory of algebraic and computational topology to analyze complex high dimensional data and develop topological techniques for various scientific tasks.

I earned a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics with a second major in Computational Science from American University (AU) in 2017. Topology was introduced to me by the applied mathematian Dr. Michael Robinson at AU, who sparked my interest in the subject, and continued to encourage and support me on my research journey in TDA.
